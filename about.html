How It All Started

My journey into communications began when I was just 12 years old. My teachers noticed something special—I had a natural love for speaking and presenting that went beyond typical classroom participation. I was always volunteering for school radio broadcasts, confidently switching between Arabic and English, and everyone could see how naturally I connected with people.

What stood out wasn't just my social nature, but my ability to generate creative, out-of-the-box ideas that others hadn't considered. This recognition helped me realize I could transform these natural talents into something bigger—a career built on the strengths that everyone had noticed in me.

"I've always prioritized making a difference. I discovered that public relations is a much broader field than most people realize—one that allows me to make an impact in any institution, regardless of its type or field."

Building the Foundation

I was fortunate to study Media at Cairo University—one of the most prestigious institutions in Africa and the Arab world, and the perfect place for me to grow. The university opened doors I never expected, providing countless opportunities to develop my skills and deepen my understanding of media and communication.

Choosing to specialize in Public Relations and Advertising felt as natural as my original decision to study media. My professors quickly noticed my ability to generate innovative marketing ideas and conduct precise research that could serve as a solid foundation for strategic campaigns. But more than that, they saw my genuine desire to make a meaningful difference.

What Drives My Approach

For marketing agencies and business owners, you know the challenge: everyone claims they can boost your brand, but few understand that real success comes from creating authentic connections. That's where my approach differs.

I see public relations as a field that's much wider than most people realize. It's not just about media coverage or social media metrics—though those matter. It's about giving your organization a distinctive human personality and building a unique identity that sets you apart from everyone else in your space.

"Every day, my passion for this field grows stronger. I discover something new, and I'm always looking for ways to contribute more and be a real addition—not just another team member."
